## What Is This Bootcamp?

This is a **complementary learning resource** that helps you:

- **Review** key concepts from lectures
- **Practice** calculations with step-by-step examples
- **Self-assess** your understanding with practice problems
- **Reference** formulas and tables quickly during study

> âš ï¸ **Important:** This bootcamp does NOT replace attending lectures or doing official exercises. It's designed to accelerate your exam preparation.

---

## Who Is This For?

This bootcamp is designed for HSG BBA students taking "Methods: Statistics" (3,120) who:

- Have attended (or are attending) the lectures
- Want structured review material
- Need extra practice with calculations
- Prefer self-paced learning

## Page Structure

Every concept page follows the same structure:

### 1. Learning Objectives
What you'll be able to do after completing the page.

### 2. Key Concepts
Theory and formulas with clear explanations.

### 3. Worked Example
Complete step-by-step solution showing:
- Formula (symbolic)
- Substitution (with values)
- Calculation
- Interpretation

### 4. Practice Problems
Problems for you to try, with solutions hidden in toggles.

### 5. Key Takeaways
Summary of the most important points.

### 6. Navigation
Links to previous/next pages and related references.

## Tips for Success

### Before You Start
- [ ] Complete the [Prerequisites Self-Assessment](prerequisites.md)
- [ ] Have pen and paper ready (or a calculator)
- [ ] Plan your study schedule

### While Studying
- [ ] Try problems BEFORE looking at solutions
- [ ] Build calculation tables like shown in examples
- [ ] Check your work with the formula glossary
- [ ] Take breaks every 45-60 minutes

### Before the Exam
- [ ] Review the [Common Mistakes](../reference/common_mistakes.md) page
- [ ] Practice with official exercise sheets
- [ ] Know where to find formulas quickly

## Getting Help

If you're stuck on a concept:
1. Re-read the theory section carefully
2. Study the worked example step-by-step
3. Try a simpler version of the problem
4. Review prerequisite concepts
5. Check the official lecture slides
