### Understanding `range(start, stop, step)`
The `range()` function in Python generates a sequence of numbers. It takes three arguments:
1. **start**: The starting value of the sequence (inclusive).
1. **stop**: The end value of the sequence (exclusive).
1. **step**: The difference between each number in the sequence.
### Analyzing `range(5, 19, 6)`
- **start = 5**: The sequence starts at 5.
- **stop = 19**: The sequence stops before reaching 19.
- **step = 6**: Each number in the sequence increases by 6 from the previous number.
### Sequence Generation
Let's see what numbers are generated by `range(5, 19, 6)`:
1. **First number**: 5 (start value)
1. **Second number**: 5 + 6 = 11
1. **Third number**: 11 + 6 = 17
The next number would be 17 + 6 = 23, but since 23 is greater than the stop value 19, it is not included in the sequence.
### Conclusion
